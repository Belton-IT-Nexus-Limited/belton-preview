AI that actually helps your team

Microsoft Copilot implementation, Power Platform, and practical AI solutions for businesses ready to work smarter.

AI is everywhere right now. Every vendor is promising it will transform your business overnight. Most of that is noise. What actually matters is finding the specific tools that help your people do their jobs better, and implementing them properly.

We focus on Microsoft's AI ecosystem because it integrates with the tools your team already uses. Copilot sits inside Word, Excel, Outlook, and Teams. Power Platform lets you automate repetitive processes without writing code. These aren't science experiments. They're practical tools that save time when they're set up correctly.

The hard part isn't the technology. It's understanding how your team actually works, identifying where AI can genuinely help, and rolling it out in a way that people will actually adopt. That's where we come in.

What we've learned

After helping dozens of organisations adopt AI tools, we've found that success comes from starting small, measuring results, and expanding what works. The businesses that try to transform everything at once usually end up with expensive licenses nobody uses.

Microsoft Copilot

Your AI assistant, built in

Copilot lives inside the Microsoft 365 apps your team uses every day. It drafts emails, summarises meetings, analyses spreadsheets, and creates presentations from your existing content. The key is that it understands your organisation's data while keeping it secure.

We handle the technical setup: licensing, security policies, data governance, and integration with your existing Microsoft 365 environment. More importantly, we help you identify the use cases that will actually deliver value for your specific workflows.

Copilot works across the Office suite. In Word and Outlook, it drafts documents and summarises email threads. In Excel, it analyses data and creates formulas from plain language requests. In Teams, it captures meeting summaries and extracts action items. In PowerPoint, it creates presentations from existing documents. All of this runs within Microsoft's enterprise security framework, so your data stays protected.

But Copilot isn't magic. It's a tool that needs proper configuration and user training to be useful. We make sure your team knows how to use it effectively, not just that they have access to it.

Licensing note

Copilot requires Microsoft 365 E3 or E5 licensing, plus the Copilot add-on. We can help you understand the cost-benefit for your organisation and identify which users will get the most value.

Not sure if it's worth it? We can run a pilot with a small team to measure actual productivity gains before you commit to a full rollout.

Power Platform

Automation without the complexity

Power Platform is Microsoft's low-code toolkit for building business applications and automating workflows. Power Automate handles repetitive tasks. Power Apps creates custom applications. Power BI turns your data into dashboards. Together, they let you solve business problems that would otherwise require custom development.

We help you identify processes that are good candidates for automation, build the solutions, and connect them to your existing systems. The goal is to eliminate manual work that's eating up your team's time.

The kinds of things we build: approval workflows that route purchase orders, leave requests, and expense claims automatically. Data entry systems where forms populate multiple applications at once, eliminating duplicate entry. Alert systems that notify the right people when specific conditions occur in your business data. Scheduled reports that generate and distribute themselves. Integration layers that connect systems that don't talk to each other natively.

These aren't toy tools. Properly implemented, they can handle complex business logic, integrate with dozens of external services, and scale as your needs grow. We've built everything from simple approval workflows to complete business process systems.

Start with one process. Pick something that consumes time and happens frequently. Automate it. Measure the time saved. Then do another. This incremental approach builds momentum and delivers value at each step.

Our approach

Practical AI, not experiments

We're not here to sell you on AI hype. We're here to help you use these tools productively. That means being honest about what they can and can't do, and focusing on implementations that deliver measurable value.

Every AI project we do starts with understanding your actual workflows. What tasks consume time? Where do errors happen? What would your team do with an extra few hours a week? We work backwards from those answers to find the right tools and approaches.

We also think about adoption. The best AI implementation is worthless if people don't use it. Training, change management, and ongoing support are part of every engagement. We measure success by whether your team actually gets value, not by how many features we've enabled.

After more than 50 Copilot deployments, we've seen teams save around 30% of their time on document-heavy work. The typical timeline from pilot to full rollout is about 4 weeks. But the numbers only matter if the tools actually get used, which is why we spend as much time on adoption as we do on technical implementation.
